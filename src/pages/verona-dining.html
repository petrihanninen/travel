01 / Three Michelin Stars

Casa
Perbellini

Verona's crown jewel. Giancarlo Perbellini's "concerto of sublime flavors" — worth a special journey.

⭐⭐⭐ Michelin 12 Apostoli Chef's table available
The Experience
Three Tasting Journeys
€230 ppFull tastingWine from €120
Io e Silvia — the personal, inventive menu. Storie di Casa — a journey through Perbellini's career-defining dishes. L'Essenza — vegetarian, gluten-free, dairy-free — cuisine in its purest form.
À La Carte Option
2 courses €2203 courses €240
You can mix dishes from the tasting menus rather than committing to one full journey. Useful if you want flexibility or a slightly shorter evening.
Wine & Practicalities

Wine Program

  • 300+ labels, strong focus on Amarone della Valpolicella DOCG
  • French gems alongside local Veneto selections
  • Wine pairing: 3 glasses €120 / 5 glasses €180

Vibe & Setting

  • Interiors designed by Patricia Urquiola — modern, intimate
  • Chef's table seats you right in the kitchen action
  • Central Verona, walkable from Piazza delle Erbe

Budget Reality

  • Tasting + wine pairing: ~€350–410 pp. Over budget, but this is the once-in-a-trip splurge
  • Three stars is three stars — you're paying for a "worth a special journey" experience
  • Book well in advance, especially weekends
02 / One Michelin Star

Il Desco

Forty years of Michelin-starred dining. Verona's most storied fine-dining institution.

⭐ Since 1985 Chef Matteo Rizzo Historic center
The Experience
Chapter 43 Tasting Menu
Signature journeyTradition meets modern
A narrative tasting menu tracing Il Desco's evolution from 1982 to today. Each course is a chapter — expect Veronese classics reinvented with modern technique and seasonal ingredients.
À La Carte Courses
3 courses €1204 courses €1355 courses €145
Flexible format — build your own meal from a rotating seasonal menu. Wine pairing runs €75–115 depending on number of glasses.
Wine & Practicalities

Wine

  • Red wine focus — strong Verona-region representation
  • Custom pairings: 3 glasses €75 / 4 glasses €95 / 5 glasses €115
  • Sommelier-guided — let them steer you toward local gems

Vibe & Setting

  • Elegant, old-world interior in a historic Verona building
  • Run by the Rizzo family — genuine warmth, not corporate polish
  • Feels like classic Italian fine dining done right

Budget Reality

  • 4 courses + wine pairing: ~€230 pp. Right at the budget limit
  • Best value of the starred options for a full evening
  • The 40-year track record means zero risk of a bad night
03 / One Michelin Star

Osteria la
Fontanina

Two centuries of history by Ponte Pietra. Michelin-starred but with a soulful, bohemian edge.

⭐ Michelin 200+ year history Near Roman Theatre
The Experience
Fish & Meat Tasting Menus
Multi-courseSeasonalClassic recipes perfected
Two tasting paths — fish or meat — built around perfected classics. Think suckling pig, slow-roasted guinea fowl, and whatever the market brought that morning. Not flashy, just deeply good.
The Atmosphere
IntimateBohemianOld Verona
Old mirrors, prints, silverware, plants — subdued lighting and small tables. The Tapparini family has run it since 1984. It feels like a place that earns your trust immediately.
Wine & Practicalities

Wine

  • Wine list leans heavily on Verona-region reds
  • Good depth in Valpolicella and Amarone selections
  • The sommelier knows the list — ask for guidance

Location

  • Santo Stefano district, steps from Ponte Pietra and the Roman Theatre
  • Beautiful walk there through old Verona — part of the experience
  • Easy to combine with an evening stroll along the Adige

Budget Reality

  • Tasting menu + wine: likely ~€150–180 pp — comfortably within budget
  • Most "Verona local" feel of any starred restaurant
  • Perfect if you want starred quality without the formality
04 / One Michelin Star — Valpolicella

Amistà

Michelin-starred dining inside the Byblos Art Hotel. A five-star evening in the Valpolicella hills.

⭐ 5 consecutive years Chef Mattia Bianchi 1,500+ wine labels
The Experience
Three Tasting Menus
6 courses €120Wine pairing €80Vegan option
Chef Bianchi sources from sustainable Valpolicella markets. Three menus including a fully vegan journey — all rooted in local terroir but presented with creative flair. The shorter dégustation runs ~€90.
The Setting
Byblos Art Hotel5-star luxuryCorrubbio
Set in an art-filled luxury hotel in the Valpolicella countryside. The whole place is curated — contemporary art everywhere, gardens, pool. Feels like a destination in itself.
Wine & Practicalities

Wine

  • 1,500+ labels — one of the richest cellars in the Verona area
  • Rare bottles and verticals alongside approachable local picks
  • Wine pairing €80 for the 6-course menu

Getting There

  • In Corrubbio, ~20 min drive from Verona center
  • Worth combining with a Valpolicella wine tour earlier in the day
  • Could also stay the night at the hotel and make it a full experience

Budget Reality

  • 6-course tasting + wine: ~€200 pp — right on budget
  • Shorter dégustation + wine: ~€130 pp — well within budget
  • Best value Michelin star in the Verona area
05 / One Michelin Star — Lake Garda

Oseleta

Michelin dining in an 18th-century villa above Lake Garda. Elegance, gardens, and a 600-label cellar.

⭐ Since 2013 Villa Cordevigo 100-hectare estate
The Experience
Three Seasonal Tasting Menus
6–9 coursesSeasonal rotationLake-inspired
My Way — 9-course full immersion. Since 2010 — 8 plates of tradition and territory. First Love — 6 courses, lighter format. All change with the seasons.
The Setting
Villa Cordevigo5-star relaisVeranda dining
An 18th-century villa on a 100-hectare estate in the hills of Cavaion Veronese. The best seats are the veranda — glass-walled with views of gardens and pool. Theatrical plating with refined technique.
Wine & Practicalities

Wine

  • 600+ labels, one of the richest cellars near Lake Garda
  • Villa Cordevigo produces its own wines on the estate
  • Strong local focus — Bardolino, Valpolicella, and Garda-area whites

Getting There

  • Cavaion Veronese — ~25 min from Verona, above Lake Garda
  • Perfect if you're combining Verona with a Lake Garda day
  • Stunning property to arrive at — consider staying overnight

Budget Reality

  • Tasting menus likely €120–180 pp before wine. Wine pairing adds ~€80–100
  • Total ~€200–280 pp depending on menu choice
  • The "First Love" 6-course is probably the sweet spot for budget

Recommendation

What I'd Do

Best bet for the evening?
Il Desco — starred since 1985, flexible courses, and ~€200 pp with wine. Zero risk.
Most soul?
La Fontanina — 200-year-old osteria, walking distance from Ponte Pietra, well under budget.
Once-in-a-lifetime splurge?
Casa Perbellini — three Michelin stars, above budget, but this is why you go to Verona.
Best value with a car?
Amistà — €120 tasting + €80 wine, stunning art hotel, and a Valpolicella day trip built in.

Next Steps

  • Book early — Casa Perbellini and Il Desco fill up fast, especially weekends
  • Mention wine interest when booking — some restaurants will prepare a tailored pairing
  • Consider a Valpolicella day — wine tastings in the afternoon, Amistà for dinner
  • Walk to dinner if eating in the center — evening Verona is gorgeous
  • Ask about the chef's table at Casa Perbellini if you want the full show
