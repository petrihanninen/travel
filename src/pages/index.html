01 / Switzerland

Bernese
Oberland

Dramatic cliffs, waterfalls, classic alpine villages. The "Alps + comfort" choice.

Excellent trains Mountain restaurants No car needed
Full-Day Hikes
Schynige Platte → First
16 km6–7 hrsRidge traverse
The classic all-day panorama hike. Constant views of Eiger, Mönch & Jungfrau. Mountain restaurants along the route. Train up, gondola down.
Mürren → Rotstockhütte → Schilthorn
14–18 km5–8 hrsCliffside trails
Vertical scenery and wild-feeling terrain. Big limestone walls, waterfalls, and an ideal mid-hike lunch stop at Rotstockhütte.
Half-Day Highlights & Practicalities

Shorter Hikes

  • Oeschinensee panoramic loop — turquoise lake + cliffs
  • Bachalpsee — classic alpine lake reflections
  • Schynige Platte flower trails — recovery day
  • Trümmelbach Falls — waterfalls inside the mountain

Food & Stay

  • Strong mountain restaurant culture with terrace views
  • Boutique hotel in Mürren or Wengen, strong breakfast + spa

Season

  • High ridges may still have snow in May / early June
  • Balcony trails and valley hikes are very reliable
  • Easy to pivot to lower hikes on bad weather days
Switzerland — Bernese Oberland

Weather in May & June

May
🌡
Temperature (at altitude)
-1°C to 7°C — still quite cold
🌧
Rain
~22 rain days, 117 mm total — wettest month
❄️
Snow
Common above 2,000 m. High ridges mostly inaccessible
🥾
Trails
Valley and balcony trails only. Most ridge hikes closed
June
🌡
Temperature
3°C to 13°C (altitude) / 7°C to 16°C (valley)
🌧
Rain
~20 rain days, 212 mm — 60% chance of rain on any day
❄️
Snow
Patchy above 2,200 m in early June. Mostly clear by late June
☀️
Daylight
15 h 48 min — sunrise 05:37, sunset 21:25
🥾
Trails
Hiking season starts mid-June. Männlichen–Scheidegg typically snow-free
Key takeaway: Late May is limited to valley hikes. By mid-to-late June, most mid-altitude trails open up. Rain gear is essential — weather changes in minutes. Use the MeteoSwiss app every morning.
02 / Iceland

South Coast
& Þórsmörk

Raw nature and big adventure days. Waterfalls, glaciers, volcanic landscapes.

Most unique nature Dramatic scale Car recommended
Full-Day Hikes
Fimmvörðuháls (Skógar → Þórsmörk)
~25 km8–10 hrsPoint-to-point
Iceland's most iconic long hike. ~26 waterfalls, glaciers, and fresh lava fields. No food en route — pack food, feast later. Feels like multiple landscapes in one day.
Þórsmörk "Double Loop" Day
15–22 kmChoose by weather
Two connected loops through canyons, birch forest, glacial rivers, and moss cliffs. Wild feeling without being technical.
Half-Day Highlights & Practicalities

Shorter Hikes

  • Reykjadalur hot spring river — walk + soak
  • Skaftafell glacier viewpoints — short + epic
  • Dyrhólaey cliffs — birds + views
  • Glymur waterfall — West Iceland

Food & Spa

  • Best meals in Reykjavík + select countryside hotels
  • Plan food as a destination, not an afterthought
  • Sky Lagoon + geothermal pools in many hotels

Season

  • Excellent: long daylight + peak waterfalls
  • Highlands often still closed (not a dealbreaker)
  • Most volatile weather — always plan contingencies
Iceland — South Coast & Þórsmörk

Weather in May & June

May
🌡
Temperature
4°C to 10°C — cool but manageable
💨
Wind
Strong, especially along the coast. Windproof layers essential
🌧
Rain
Moderate but unpredictable — "four seasons in one day"
❄️
Snow / Access
Highlands still closed. Highland roads (F-roads) impassable
☀️
Daylight
~20 hours — near-midnight sun begins
June
🌡
Temperature
7°C to 12°C on South Coast
💨
Wind
Average 8 m/s — still breezy, less intense than winter
🌧
Rain
~50 mm, ~11 rain days — mix of sunny spells and showers
❄️
Snow / Access
Low elevations clear. Glaciers and highland passes may still be closed
☀️
Daylight
Effectively 24 hours — midnight sun, never fully dark
Key takeaway: Iceland is the most weather-volatile destination. June is significantly better than May for access and conditions. Fimmvörðuháls requires checking conditions — snow on the pass is common into June. Always have wind/rain contingency plans. The endless daylight is a genuine advantage.
03 / Dolomites

Alta Badia &
Val Gardena

Scenic long hikes and the best mountain food anywhere. Rifugi lunches as a daily event.

Best food on trail Dramatic towers Spa hotels
Full-Day Hikes
Seceda Ridge → Val de Funes
~17 km6–8 hrsRidge + meadows
The "Dolomites postcard" day. Knife-edge ridge views, spires, meadows, and multiple rifugi for food and coffee along the way.
Lagazuoi → Cinque Torri → Averau
14–16 km5–7 hrsRock towers
Famous formations and wide-open views. Rifugio Averau and Nuvolau are standout food stops with stunning terraces.
Half-Day Highlights & Practicalities

Shorter Hikes

  • Alpe di Siusi meadow loops — early-season friendly
  • Lago di Sorapis — turquoise lake, go early
  • Tre Cime base loop — iconic, manage crowds
  • Val di Funes village viewpoints — huge payoff

Food & Stay

  • Best food-on-the-trail of any destination
  • Excellent spa hotels, especially Alta Badia
  • Breakfast quality is generally high

Season

  • Late May can be patchy above ~2,200 m
  • Early June is usually good for mid-altitude routes
  • Afternoon storms possible — start early
Dolomites — Alta Badia / Val Gardena

Weather in May & June

May
🌡
Temperature (mid-elevation)
10°C to 18°C — pleasant in valleys, cold higher up
🌧
Rain
Moderate — spring showers common
❄️
Snow
Significant above 2,000–2,200 m. Meltwater and unstable surfaces
⚠️
Hazards
Avalanche risk on steep slopes. Rifugi closed all month
🥾
Trails
Valley walks and low-altitude only. High routes closed
June
🌡
Temperature
12°C to 22°C (valley) — comfortable hiking weather
⛈
Storms
Afternoon thunderstorms common — start hikes early
❄️
Snow
Melts below 2,600 m by mid-June. Lingers on shaded north slopes
🏔
Rifugi
Some open from second week of June. Full opening from late June
🥾
Trails
Mid-altitude routes good from mid-June. Hut-to-hut possible late June
Key takeaway: May is essentially pre-season — huts closed, snow everywhere above 2,000 m. The last 10 days of June are the sweet spot: rifugi open, mid-altitude trails clear, fewer crowds than July. Always start early to beat afternoon thunderstorms.
04 / Austrian Alps

Stubai, Ötztal
& Innsbruck

Easy logistics, hut culture, and spa hotels. Classic Alps at great value.

Best value Well-marked trails Best spa options
Full-Day Hikes
Schlicker Seespitze Circuit (Stubai)
~15 km6–8 hrsRidge + summit
A full alpine day without extreme exposure. Ridge and summit feel that's very manageable, with hut stops along the way.
Ötztal High Valley Traverse
~18 km6–8 hrsGlacier scenery
Big valley scale with glacier scenery. Long but non-technical with frequent hut infrastructure for breaks and food.
Half-Day Highlights & Practicalities

Shorter Hikes

  • Zirbenweg (Innsbruck) — panoramic balcony trail
  • Gorge walks (Ötztal) — dramatic + short
  • Glacier viewpoint walks — Stubai Glacier area
  • Easy hut walks — "lunch as the goal" days

Food & Spa

  • Hut food is excellent and frequent
  • Tyrolean dishes are very satisfying after big days
  • Easiest destination for spa hotels

Season

  • Many great hikes open early — lower valleys
  • High ridges may still have snow in late May
  • Generally more stable weather than Switzerland
Austrian Alps — Tyrol

Weather in May & June

May
🌡
Temperature
8°C to 18°C in valleys — pleasant in the sun
🌧
Rain
Moderate spring showers, improving through the month
❄️
Snow
Melting rapidly at lower elevations. Still heavy above 2,200 m
💧
Waterfalls
Peak snowmelt = peak flow. Stuibenfall at its most dramatic
🥾
Trails
Valley trails fully open by late May. First huts begin opening
June
🌡
Temperature
11°C to 23°C — warm, comfortable hiking days
🌧
Rain
Occasional afternoon showers. Generally more stable than Switzerland
❄️
Snow
Clearing at mid-altitudes. Stubai Glacier is snow-covered year-round
🌸
Wildflowers
Alpine meadows peak bloom mid-June: gentians, alpine roses, edelweiss
🥾
Trails
Most mid-altitude trails open. High alpine routes (Stubai Höhenweg) from late June
Key takeaway: Austria offers the earliest start of these alpine destinations — valley hikes work from late May. June is reliably warm with fewer rain days than Switzerland. High alpine routes like the Stubai Höhenweg need late June. Wildflower timing in mid-June is a real bonus.
05 / Slovenia

Julian Alps
& Lake Bohinj

The hidden gem. Turquoise rivers, forests, lakes, and alpine ridges — less crowds, great value.

Hidden gem Dense highlights Great value
Full-Day Hikes
Seven Lakes Valley
~17 km6–8 hrsLakes + karst
Slovenia's signature alpine day. Lakes, karst scenery, and huts. Feels like a mini-adventure without huge altitude.
Pokljuka Gorge → Lake Bohinj
~15 km5–7 hrsGorge to lake
Forested gorge transitioning to a big lake finish. Very satisfying point-to-point feel through varied terrain.
Half-Day Highlights & Practicalities

Shorter Hikes

  • Vintgar Gorge — short + dramatic
  • Savica & Peričnik Waterfalls — iconic
  • Lake Bohinj shoreline walks
  • Soča Valley — turquoise river viewpoints (day trip)

Food & Stay

  • Better than expected: local trout, stews, pastries
  • Great wine for non-hiking evenings
  • Lakeside stays are excellent for breakfast

Season

  • Great time to visit — green, waterfalls strong
  • Higher trails may still be snowy in May
  • Easy to pivot to gorges and lakes on bad days
Slovenia — Julian Alps

May
🌡
Temperature
Up to 21°C at lake level. Drops 9–10°C per 1,000 m of elevation
🌧
Rain
Tail end of wet season. Moderate, with some longer spells possible
❄️
Snow
Expect snow above 2,000 m, especially on north-facing slopes
🥾
Trails
Low and mid-altitude trails accessible. Gorges and lakeside walks great
June
🌡
Temperature
Avg high 19°C in Julian Alps — warm and pleasant
🌧
Rain
Moderate, brief showers. Often refreshing, not all-day rain
❄️
Snow
Lingers in shady high spots even in early summer. Mostly clear below 2,000 m
🌸
Flowers

🥾
Trails
Most trails open. High-altitude huts open from late June
Key takeaway: Slovenia's lower altitude means earlier access than many alpine rivals. May is already very hikeable at lake and gorge level. June is excellent — warm days, green valleys, strong waterfalls from snowmelt. Seven Lakes Valley may need late June for full snow clearance.
06 / French Alps

Chamonix &
Beaufortain

Big mountains and strong food culture. Mont Blanc scenery at its finest.

Mont Blanc views Strong dining scene No car needed
Full-Day Hikes
Lac Blanc Circuit
~15 km6–7 hrsMont Blanc views
The best single-day hike in the Chamonix area. Mont Blanc reflections, glacier views, and a refuge lunch right at the lake.
Tour du Mont Blanc — Day Stage
16–20 km6–9 hrsTMB experience
The TMB experience without committing to 10 days. Continuous alpine scenery, huts, and a great "big day out" feeling.
Half-Day Highlights & Practicalities

Shorter Hikes

  • Mer de Glace viewpoints — glacier access
  • Gorges de la Diosaz — waterfalls + canyon
  • Beaufortain balcony trails — quiet + scenic
  • Easy valley walks with constant Mont Blanc views

Food & Spa

  • Strongest town dining scene after Switzerland
  • Great cheese, pastries, and wine culture
  • Many hotels have saunas/spas + thermal options

Season

  • Many high trails still snowy in May
  • June is much more reliable
  • Fast weather changes, but plenty of backup hikes
French Alps — Chamonix

Weather in May & June

May
🌡
Temperature
Valley: warm days possible. Passes: 5–15°C with wind chill
🌧
Rain
Spring showers common. Unsettled weather patterns
❄️
Snow
Heavy above 2,000 m. Rockfall risk from melting snow — walk with care
🥾
Trails
Valley floor and low-altitude walks only. High trails snow-covered
June
🌡
Temperature
15–25°C in valleys, 5–15°C on passes (colder if wet/windy)
🌧
Rain
Moderate — afternoon showers possible, fast weather changes
❄️
Snow
Can linger on passes into late June. Icy from melt-refreeze cycles
⚠️
Note
Check with High Mountain Office before heading out. Conditions vary year to year
🥾
Trails
Lac Blanc circuit may open mid-June. TMB stages from late June
Key takeaway: Chamonix's high altitude means later snow clearance than most destinations here. May is essentially pre-season for hiking. Mid-to-late June is the realistic start — but after a heavy winter, snow can persist on passes into July. Always check conditions with the Office de Haute Montagne.
