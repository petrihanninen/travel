01 / The Viewpoint

Punta Larici
from Pregasina

A natural balcony 900 m above the lake. Arguably the single best panorama on all of Garda.

Best viewpoint Forest road + ridge Exposed final section
The Hike
Pregasina → Punta Larici (loop via Malga Palaer)
~6 km loop2.5–3 hrsModerate–Exposed
Start from the little parking lot below the church in Pregasina. A gentle forest road (trail 422b) winds through woods to Malga Palaer, then up to Bocca Larici. From there, a short 5–10 minute spur reaches the actual viewpoint — and that's where it gets serious. The final stretch runs along exposed rock edges with cable hand-holds. Not technical, but not for anyone nervous about drops.
What You See
From the point: Malcesine and Brenzone on the Monte Baldo slopes across the water, Limone directly below, Riva and Torbole to the north, Monte Stivo behind them. On a clear day, it feels like you can see the entire lake from a single spot. There's a reason locals call it the most spectacular lookout on Garda.
Good to Know

Intensity

  • The forest road section is easy and shaded — great warm-up
  • The final spur to the viewpoint is classed EE (expert hikers) due to exposure
  • Cable hand-holds are solid, but it's not a place to bring kids or go in rain
  • 370 m elevation gain total — moderately demanding

Snack Strategy

  • No cafes or refuges on this route — bring everything with you
  • The forest clearing at Malga Palaer is a nice picnic spot mid-hike
  • Pregasina village has a small bar for a post-hike drink
  • Or drive 10 min down to Riva del Garda for a proper lakefront aperitivo

Late June Notes

  • Morning start recommended — the viewpoint is fully exposed to afternoon sun and storms
  • The forest sections stay cool, but the final ridge can be hot and windy
  • Sunrise from here is supposed to be exceptional, if you're up for a pre-dawn start
02 / The Cliff Trail

Pieve to
Campione

Steep ravines, carved rock steps, illuminated tunnels, and a panoramic terrace with coffee at the top. The adventure walk.

Tremosine plateau Rock-carved steps Terrace cafe at top
The Hike
Campione del Garda → Pieve di Tremosine
~8 km3–4 hrsModerate–Demanding
Starts at lake level in Campione and climbs 490 m through a narrow ravine with stratified cliff walls. The path includes rock-carved steps, wire-rope secured sections, and even short illuminated tunnels carved through the mountain. Proper mountain trail — not a stroll. The payoff is Pieve di Tremosine, a village perched on the cliff edge with a panoramic terrace that is genuinely jaw-dropping.
Why It's Worth the Effort
This is the most varied walk on the list. You go from lakeside Mediterranean vegetation through a sheer ravine into mountain forest and emerge on a cliff-edge village. The views of Monte Baldo across the lake get better with every switchback. It feels properly adventurous without needing climbing gear.
Cafe at the Top & Practicalities

The Reward

  • Pieve di Tremosine has a panoramic terrace overhanging the lake — best cappuccino spot in the area
  • Several small restaurants in the village for a proper sit-down lunch
  • The terrace view alone is worth the climb, even on a hazy day

Important Warnings

  • Trail marked as "experienced hikers only" — take that seriously
  • Falling rock risk in the ravine — a helmet is officially recommended
  • Wire-rope sections can be slippery when wet — skip this on rainy days
  • Proper hiking boots essential, not trail runners

Logistics

  • Bus back from Pieve to Campione, or arrange pickup — you don't want to descend the same way
  • Can also start from the top (Pieve) and descend — easier on the legs, harder on the knees
  • Start early — the ravine loses sun by mid-afternoon and the trail gets darker
03 / The Adventure Loop

Tibetan Bridge
& Olive Groves

A suspended bridge 42 m above a valley, prehistoric rock carvings, and olive grove paths. Torri del Benaco's hidden circuit.

Torri del Benaco Suspension bridge Rock engravings
The Walk
Torri del Benaco → Crero → Tibetan Bridge → Albisano Loop
~10 km loop3.5–4 hrsModerate
From Torri del Benaco, climb through olive groves to the hamlet of Crero with its ancient church of San Siro. A dirt road continues through olive trees to the Ponte Tibetano — a 35-metre steel bridge suspended 42 m above the Val Vanzana stream. Cross it, continue on trail 38 toward San Zeno di Montagna, then loop back through Albisano on the Pilgrim's Path past prehistoric rock carvings. About 600 m elevation gain total.
Short Version: Just the Bridge
~3 km return1–1.5 hrsEasy
If you just want the bridge experience, park at Crero and walk the 20-minute dirt road through olive groves to the bridge and back. Quick, easy, and still a thrill. The bridge is limited to 15 people at a time, so there's no crowding.
Why This Walk Is Special

The Unique Bits

  • The bridge itself is a proper adrenaline moment — non-slip surface, safe, but 42 m above the valley floor
  • Prehistoric rock engravings on the Pilgrim's Path are thousands of years old — unexpected cultural depth
  • Crero and Albisano are tiny hamlets most tourists never reach
  • Olive groves on the east shore have a different character from the west — drier, more open

Snacks & Wine

  • Torri del Benaco waterfront has excellent cafes and wine bars for post-hike
  • The Scaliger castle in Torri houses a lemon house and small museum — nice wind-down
  • Bring a picnic for the olive grove sections — benches with views along the way

Late June Notes

  • East shore gets morning sun first — start early or the olive grove sections bake
  • The bridge and valley sections are shaded and cooler
  • Torri del Benaco is less crowded than Malcesine or Sirmione — a quieter base
04 / The Wine Walk

Bardolino
Vineyard Trails

61 wineries connected by 18 marked trails through vineyards and olive groves. Walk a bit, taste a bit, repeat.

Winery stops Vineyard views QR-code route signs
The Walk
The Way of Bardolino (choose your loop)
3–20 km options1.5–4 hrsEasy
A 100 km network of 18 signposted trails through six municipalities: Bardolino, Costermano, Garda, Rivoli Veronese, Affi, and Cavaion Veronese. Pick a loop that suits your mood. The shorter 3–5 km routes are basically wine strolls — gently rolling hills through vineyards and olive groves with lake views and winery stops along the way. The longer routes climb into the morainic hills for bigger panoramas.
The Concept
Created in 2020, the project links 61 local wineries with 53 info signs equipped with QR codes for route details and local history. Picnic areas with benches are dotted along the trails, and free parking at multiple trailheads. This isn't a hike — it's a curated wander through working wine country with legitimate tasting opportunities at every turn.
Wine & Food Strategy

What to Taste

  • Bardolino Chiaretto — the local rosé, perfect chilled on a warm day
  • Bardolino DOC red — light, fruity, almost Beaujolais-like. Great summer red
  • Many wineries do olive oil tastings alongside the wine
  • Some offer food pairings: local salami, cheese, bread with their olive oil

Recommended Approach

  • Pick a 5–8 km morning loop, hit 2–3 wineries max (you're walking back)
  • End in Bardolino town center for a lakefront lunch
  • Saturday mornings: the Bardolino market is excellent for picking up picnic supplies first
  • This pairs well with a "rest day" between more intense hikes

Late June Notes

  • Vines are fully leafed out and photogenic — peak vineyard beauty
  • Start by 9am to hit wineries when they open and avoid midday heat
  • The gentle hills offer minimal shade — hat and sunscreen essential
  • Many wineries close for a few hours at lunch — plan around that
05 / The Hidden Gem

San Valentino
Hermitage

A tiny church built under a rock overhang high above Gargnano. Short, steep, and surprisingly dramatic.

Gargnano Rock hermitage Steep but short
The Hike
Sasso → Eremo di San Valentino (circular)
~5 km2–2.5 hrsModerate–Steep
Starts from the hamlet of Sasso above Gargnano. A short but steep climb with some equipped sections (cable hand-holds, nothing technical) leads to a rustic chapel literally built under a massive rock overhang. Natural terraces around the hermitage open up to views over the entire lake — Gargnano directly below, the eastern shore stretching north. It's the kind of place that feels discovered, not visited.
For the Ambitious: Continue to Cima Comer
~10 km4–5 hrsDemanding
The trail continues past the hermitage up to Cima Comer at 1,279 m. Properly demanding but turns a half-day walk into a full mountain experience. Only worth it if you want the extra elevation and have the legs for it — the hermitage alone is already the highlight.
Gargnano & Practicalities

Why Gargnano

  • Quieter and more authentic than the big-name lake towns
  • DH Lawrence lived here — described it as the most beautiful place on the lake
  • The waterfront is understated and local, not resort-ified
  • Excellent small restaurants without the tourist markup

Snacks & Wine Plan

  • No facilities at the hermitage — bring water and a snack for the viewpoint
  • The real reward is lunch in Gargnano afterward: lakeside, unhurried, local fish
  • Try Lugana or Groppello (a rare local red from the west shore) with your meal

Late June Notes

  • The steep sections can be hot — morning start non-negotiable
  • The rock overhang at the hermitage provides welcome shade for a rest
  • Gargnano's waterfront faces east — afternoon shade arrives earlier, nice for a late lunch
06 / The Easy One

Malcesine to
Cassone

A flat lakeside stroll through the Valley of Dreams, past two islands and the world's shortest river. The wine-glass walk.

Flat & lakeside Val di Sogno Waterfront cafes
The Walk
Malcesine → Val di Sogno → Cassone
~4 km one way1.5–2 hrsEasy
From Malcesine old town, follow the lakeside path south through Val di Sogno — "Valley of Dreams" — a scattered stretch of villas, watersports stations, and quiet swimming spots. You pass two of Garda's five islands: Isola dell'Olivo and Isola del Sogno. Cassone at the end is a tiny hamlet built around the Aril, officially the world's shortest river at just 175 metres. A perfectly preserved harbour with colourful fishing boats and a medieval tower.
The Vibe
This is the walk for when you want views and charm without any effort at all. Zero elevation gain, constant lake views, and you end up somewhere with a harbour cafe and a glass of wine. It's also an ideal late-afternoon walk when the light on the water is best and the day's heat is fading.
The Wine-Glass Walk

Cafe & Wine Stops

  • Malcesine old town has a dozen wine bars and cafes along the waterfront
  • Val di Sogno has a couple of low-key beach bars with loungers
  • Cassone harbour: tiny, quiet, genuinely local — sit by the fishing boats with a Lugana
  • Walk back (or bus) and finish with aperitivo in Malcesine's piazza

Combine With

  • Morning: Monte Baldo cable car (the other Lake Garda page has details)
  • Afternoon: this walk when you're back down — perfect pairing
  • Malcesine's Scaliger castle is worth 30 min if you haven't been
  • The Aril river in Cassone is charming — tiny bridges, ducks, gardens

Late June Notes

  • Afternoon lake breezes make this comfortable even on hot days
  • Swimming spots along Val di Sogno — bring swimwear in your bag
  • Lake water hits 22–24°C by late June — genuinely pleasant

The pattern: Mornings are clear, calm, and the best time for everything. By early afternoon, thermal winds build and thunderstorms roll in — especially over the mountains. Don't get caught on Punta Larici or the Tremosine cliffs after 1pm. The lakeside walks (Malcesine–Cassone, Bardolino vineyards) are safer in afternoon heat. Always carry a rain shell. The storms pass fast — 30 minutes and it's often blue sky again.
Weather by Hike

What to Watch For

Punta Larici
Fully exposed ridge — avoid afternoon storms at all costs. Morning only.
Pieve–Campione
Rock surfaces slippery when wet. Wire ropes unreliable in rain. Dry days only.
Tibetan Bridge Loop
Olive groves bake in midday sun. Bridge area shaded. Morning start, bridge is the cool midpoint.
Bardolino Vineyards
Minimal shade. Fine in morning or late afternoon. Can handle light rain — vineyards don't care.
San Valentino
Steep exposed climb. Rock overhang provides shelter at top if caught in a shower.
Malcesine–Cassone
Lakeside and low. Works any time. Best in late afternoon light and evening breeze.

How I'd Do It

These walks complement the other Garda hikes page. Mix and match — you could easily fill a week alternating between the two lists without repeating a trail or a lunch spot.

Morning Power Walk

Punta Larici at dawn. The viewpoint is supposed to be extraordinary at sunrise. Back in Riva by 10am for a big coffee and pastry. Afternoon swim.

Adventure Day

Campione → Pieve di Tremosine early morning. Cappuccino on the panoramic terrace at the top. Bus back down. This is the one that earns you a big lunch.

Active Rest Day

Tibetan Bridge short loop in the morning from Crero (1.5 hrs). Afternoon: Malcesine–Cassone lakeside stroll with a swim at Val di Sogno. Aperitivo in Malcesine.

Wine Day

Bardolino vineyard loop — pick a 5–8 km route, hit 2 wineries, end in town for lakefront lunch. This is the day you don't need hiking boots.

Culture & Views

San Valentino Hermitage in the morning from Gargnano. Steep but short. Long lakefront lunch in Gargnano — arguably the most authentic food town on the lake.
