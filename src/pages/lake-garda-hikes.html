01 / North Shore

Riva &
Torbole

Dramatic cliffs plunging straight into the lake. The most spectacular views on the water.

Cliff paths Iron stairways Panoramic terraces
North Shore Hikes
Busatte–Tempesta Trail
~9 km loop2.5–3 hrsModerate
The single most popular hike on the lake, and for good reason. Over 400 iron steps bolted into cliff faces, suspended walkways over sheer drops, and constant eye-level views across the water to the western shore. Feels mildly via-ferrata without the gear.
Sentiero del Ponale
~10 km3–4 hrsModerate
Old military road carved into the cliff above Riva, climbing through hairpin bends to the Ledro Valley. The Madonna terrace viewpoint about halfway up is jaw-dropping. More of a steady climb than Busatte — you earn the views with 600 m of elevation gain.
Also Worth It & Practicalities

Monte Brione (2–3 hrs)

  • Gentle climb through olive groves and Mediterranean scrub
  • 360° views from the top — lake to the south, mountains everywhere else
  • Old Austrian fortress ruins add some exploring
  • Easier and shadier than the cliff trails — good for a warm day

Pit Stops

  • Riva old town has plenty of lakefront cafes for after-hike wine
  • Ponale trail: bring a snack — the Ledro Valley end has small bars
  • Busatte-Tempesta: pack a picnic, there's a park at the trailhead

Notes for Late June

  • North end catches afternoon winds — morning starts essential
  • Ponale gets full sun midday; bring hat and plenty of water
  • Busatte iron steps can be slippery after rain — check forecast
02 / Northwest Shore

Limone &
Tignale

Olive groves, waterfalls, and the scent of bougainvillea. The gentler, more Mediterranean side of the lake.

Olive grove trails Waterfalls Picnic heaven
Northwest Hikes
Sentiero del Sole (Sun Trail)
9 km3–4 hrsEasy-Moderate
Winds through ancient olive trees and bougainvillea with optional detour to Sopino Waterfall. This is the one to bring a bottle of wine and some cheese for — find a shaded olive grove bench with lake views and call it a morning. The trail also has a suspended walkway section right over the water.
Tignale Waterfall Trails
6–7 km2.5–3 hrsModerate
Multiple waterfall viewpoints through cool forest — refreshing when it's 28°C. Some of the cascade pools are swimmable. The forest canopy keeps you shaded most of the way, and Tignale village at the end has a couple of small bars for a well-earned Aperol.
Picnic & Wine Strategy

Best Picnic Setup

  • Sentiero del Sole is the obvious winner — bring everything with you
  • Olive grove benches with lake backdrop are numerous along the trail
  • Sopino Waterfall detour has a natural picnic area by the cascade
  • Tignale waterfalls: pack light snacks, save appetite for the village

Cafe & Bar Options

  • Limone sul Garda town center: multiple lakefront spots for wine
  • Tignale village: small authentic bars, not touristy
  • Consider a morning hike → waterfront lunch → afternoon swim pattern

Notes for Late June

  • Sentiero del Sole gets hot midday — start by 8am or go late afternoon
  • Tignale waterfalls are cooler and work better for a midday hike
  • Olive grove sections have partial shade but bring sun protection
03 / West Shore

Salò &
Gardone

Pine-shaded hills above elegant lakeside towns. Hike in the morning, long lunch by the water.

Pine forests Gulf views Great lunch towns
West Shore Hikes
Monte San Bartolomeo — La Corna Viewpoint
4–5 km2–2.5 hrsEasy-Moderate
Pine forest trail climbing gently from Salò to the La Corna viewpoint at 350 m. The gulf views from the top are outstanding — the whole southern basin laid out below you. Shaded nearly the entire way, making it ideal for warmer days. Quick enough to finish before lunch in town.
Gardone Riviera → Gardone Sopra
5–6 km2.5–3 hrsModerate
From the lakeside promenade up through terraced hillsides to the upper village. The contrast is the point — Belle Époque resort architecture below, quiet stone hamlet above. Gardone Sopra has a handful of village restaurants that don't see many tourists. Plan lunch there.
The Lunch-Hike Combo

Why This Area Works

  • Both hikes are short enough to finish by noon
  • Salò has the best lakefront dining scene on the western shore
  • Gardone Sopra's village trattorias are a hidden gem — local food, quiet
  • The Vittoriale degli Italiani gardens in Gardone are worth a post-hike visit

Wine & Snacks

  • Pick up local Lugana white at any enoteca in Salò — perfect chilled picnic wine
  • La Corna viewpoint has flat rocks — bring bread, cheese, and a bottle
  • Gardone Sopra: eat at the village, don't carry — it's better fresh up there

Notes for Late June

  • Pine forest on San Bartolomeo keeps things 3–4°C cooler than open trails
  • Gardone climb has some exposed sections — morning is better
  • Both areas are more sheltered from afternoon lake winds than the north
04 / South Shore

Sirmione &
Manerba

Gentle morainic hills, nature reserves, and the most leisurely walking on the lake. Bring wine, not hiking poles.

Nature reserves Easy terrain Lakeside picnics
South Shore Walks
Rocca di Manerba Nature Reserve
6–7 km2–3 hrsEasy-Moderate
A protected headland jutting into the southern lake. Well-marked paths wind through Mediterranean scrub to a rocky promontory with views across the whole basin. More of a nature walk than a mountain hike — wildflowers everywhere in late June. Excellent picnic territory.
Padenghe–Sirmione Trail
9 km3 hrsEasy
A longer but very flat walk through morainic hills along the southern shore. Connects two charming towns through olive groves and rocky shoreline stretches. Only 150 m elevation gain total — this is the one for a leisurely morning when you want distance without effort.
The Leisurely Option

Why Include the South

  • Recovery day walking — low effort, high charm
  • Best swimming access on the lake from rocky coves below Manerba
  • Sirmione peninsula itself is worth half a day (thermal baths, Scaliger castle)
  • Wine country starts just south — easy to combine with vineyard visits

Wine & Food

  • This is Lugana DOC territory — stop at a winery on the way back
  • Manerba village has small trattorias with lake fish specialties
  • Sirmione: touristy but the gelato and aperitivo scene is hard to resist
  • Pack a picnic for Rocca di Manerba — the headland views deserve a long stop

Notes for Late June

  • Southern shore is warmer than north — 2–3°C hotter on average
  • Less shade than northern hikes; early starts or late afternoon walks
  • Lake swimming is perfect here — water hits 22–24°C by late June
05 / East Shore

Monte Baldo
Ridge

Take the cable car up, walk a ridge 1,760 m above the lake. Alpine meadows, rare wildflowers, and the biggest views of all.

Cable car access Alpine meadows 800+ plant species
Monte Baldo Walks
Monte Baldo Ridge Walk (via cable car)
4–6 km2–3 hrsEasy (after cable car)
The Malcesine cable car drops you at 1,760 m in 10 minutes. From there, ridge paths fan out in multiple directions through what botanists call the "Garden of Europe" — over 800 rare plant species, many found nowhere else. The views straight down to the lake are vertigo-inducing. Late June is peak wildflower season.
Ridge to Rifugio Telegrafo
~8 km3–4 hrsModerate
For a fuller day, head south along the ridge to Rifugio Telegrafo. Real mountain refuge with food and drinks — sit on the terrace with a beer and half the Italian Alps on the horizon. More exposed and undulating than the shorter loops. Cable car back down from Bocca di Navene.
The High-Altitude Option

Why Monte Baldo Stands Out

  • Only hike here that feels properly alpine — and you don't earn the elevation
  • 10–15°C cooler at the top than lakeshore — perfect escape on hot days
  • Late June is the absolute peak for wildflower displays
  • Views encompass the entire lake, Dolomites to the north, Po Valley to the south

Cafe & Rifugio Stops

  • Rifugio Telegrafo: proper mountain refuge with food and terrace views
  • Rifugio Marchetti: another option on the ridge, simpler but atmospheric
  • Cable car base in Malcesine: wine bars along the old town waterfront

Practical Notes

  • Cable car runs every 30 min — book morning slots in advance for summer
  • Bring a light jacket — summit temperatures drop to 12–15°C even in July
  • Thunderstorms are more dangerous here than at lake level — watch the sky
  • Malcesine old town below is charming for a post-hike wander

The golden rule: Start hiking by 7–8am. Mornings are clear, calm, and cool. By early afternoon, thermal winds pick up and thunderstorms roll in — especially in the mountains. These storms can be sudden and violent with hail, so don't get caught on exposed ridges after 1pm. Pack a light rain shell regardless.

Recommended Itinerary

If you have 4–5 hiking days around the lake, here's how I'd sequence them — balancing intensity, variety, and wine consumption.

Day 1 — The Showstopper

Busatte–Tempesta in the morning. Iron stairs, cliff views, the works. Lunch on the Riva del Garda waterfront. Afternoon swim.

Day 2 — The Olive Grove Picnic

Sentiero del Sole from Limone. Bring Lugana wine, bread, local cheese. Long picnic in an olive grove. Optional waterfall detour at Sopino.

Day 3 — Go High

Cable car up Monte Baldo. Ridge walk to Rifugio Telegrafo for lunch. Wildflowers in every direction. Down to Malcesine for evening aperitivo.

Day 4 — Hike + Lunch Town

San Bartolomeo in the morning from Salò. Quick, shaded, great views. Long lakefront lunch in Salò — the best food town on the west shore.

Day 5 — Easy Day & Wine

Rocca di Manerba nature reserve in the morning. Gentle walk, wildflowers, swimming from the coves. Afternoon: Lugana winery visit south of the lake.
