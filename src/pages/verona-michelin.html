01 / The Three-Star

Casa Perbellini
12 Apostoli

Verona's only three-star. Giancarlo Perbellini's classicist cuisine in a century-old landmark redesigned by Patricia Urquiola.

★★★ Creative Chef's Table
Casa Perbellini 12 Apostoli
The Restaurant
★★★Creative€€€€
Named after twelve merchants that 18th-century Veronese wit nicknamed "the twelve apostles." Perbellini started his career in this kitchen, returned as chef patron, and turned it into the only three-star in the Veneto outside Venice. Three rooms — the Volto, the Affreschi, and the Chef's Table, which is basically a theatre kitchen.

Why It Matters

  • Cuisine is generous and flavour-first — no cerebral excess
  • Never more than a few ingredients per plate, all speaking clearly
  • Interiors by Patricia Urquiola blend centuries of history with razor-sharp modernity
  • Book the Chef's Table for the full performance
Vicolo Corticella San Marco 3, Verona · casaperbellini.com
02 / The Two-Star
Famiglia Rana
★★Creative€€€€Oppeano
Yes, that Rana — the pasta family. But don't expect tortellini. Chef Francesco Sodano, a Campanian who trained under Genovese and Glowig, earned two stars in just 16 months here. The setting is wild: a nature oasis with farmyard, stables, organic garden, and orchard.

The Menu

  • "Ricomincio da tre" — 12 courses tracing Sodano's journey from Campania to Veneto
  • "The Doors" — the experimental one, built on fermentation and acidity
  • "Vegetale" — entirely from the on-site garden
  • Mediterranean meets Asian, anchored by whatever's growing outside
Oppeano (20 min from Verona) · ristorantefamigliarana.it
03 / One Star
Iris Ristorante
★Contemporary€€€€Verona
Giacomo Sacchetto was sous chef to both Niederkofler and Perbellini, then got his own star within two years of opening. Set inside Palazzo Soave — appetisers are served in a 14th-century wine cellar with Roman foundations before you move to the main dining room.

What Stands Out

  • Strong sustainability focus — regional, seasonal, green-leaning
  • Adriatic seafood features prominently alongside mountain produce
  • Over 800 wines, heavy on France and Veneto
  • Two tasting menus: "Tell Me" and "Adriatic and Surroundings"
Palazzo Soave, Verona · irisristorante.it
04 / One Star
Il Desco
★Italian Contemporary€€€€Verona
Verona's longest-running star. Elia Rizzo opened it in 1981, got the star in 1985, and never lost it. Now his son Matteo runs the kitchen — trained under Locatelli in London and Selvaggio in LA. Set in a Renaissance palace in the old town.

What Stands Out

  • 40+ years of continuous Michelin recognition — the real deal
  • Matteo's rule: never more than three main ingredients per plate
  • Rooted in Veronese tradition, refined with international technique
  • A la carte from €120 for 3 courses, with wine pairings from €75
Via Dietro San Sebastiano 7, Verona · ristoranteildesco.it
05 / One Star
Amistà
★Italian Contemporary€€€€Corrubbio
Inside the Byblos Art Hotel — a 5-star villa in the Valpolicella hills where contemporary art meets fine dining. Chef Mattia Bianchi works the region's produce with unexpected oriental twists. The wine list alone is worth the trip: 1,500+ labels.

What Stands Out

  • Five consecutive years of Michelin recognition
  • Valpolicella ingredients with Asian inflections — subtle, not gimmicky
  • The hotel itself is an art gallery — dining is part of the experience
  • A natural pairing with Amarone tasting if you're in wine country
Byblos Art Hotel, Corrubbio (15 min from Verona) · ristoranteamista.com
06 / One Star
Oseleta
★Creative€€€Cavaion Veronese
Sardinian chef Marco Marras in a 5-star wine relais on the hills above Lake Garda. Villa Cordevigo is surrounded by vineyards and olive groves — the ingredients are basically in the garden. Star since 2013, with a 600-label cellar.

What Stands Out

  • Three seasonal tasting menus: "My Way" (9 courses), "Since 2010" (8), "First Love" (6)
  • Best tables are on the veranda overlooking the gardens and pool
  • Sardinia-meets-Lake Garda fusion — seafood, lake fish, and local game
  • Perfect if you're combining Verona dining with a Garda stay
Villa Cordevigo, Cavaion Veronese (25 min from Verona) · ristoranteoseleta.it
07 / One Star
Damini Macelleria & Affini
★Meats & Grills€€€Arzignano
You walk in through a butcher shop. Literally. The first and only Michelin-starred macelleria in Europe. Brothers Gian Pietro and Giorgio Damini turned their family butcher's into a restaurant where every cut is aged in-house — 40 to 100 days for wagyu and gallega.

What Stands Out

  • The entrance doubles as a deli and food shop — buy provisions on the way out
  • Hand-cut tartare and the celebrated Damini hamburger
  • Not just steak — creative dishes using every part of the animal
  • The most unique concept on this list by far
Arzignano (50 min from Verona) · daminieaffini.com
08 / One Star
Casin del Gamba
★Regional€€€€Altissimo
A former hunting lodge on a steep mountain road, run by the same family since 1976 and starred since 1992. Chef Antonio Dal Lago cooks what the mountain gives him — trout and char from Altissimo, wild mushrooms, game in winter, aromatic herbs from the forest.

What Stands Out

  • Most "terroir" restaurant on the list — ingredients are hyper-local
  • The drive up is an experience in itself — winding Lessinia mountain roads
  • Game and mushroom season (autumn/winter) is the peak time
  • Local snails with thyme and onion bread — a signature
Altissimo (55 min from Verona) · casindelgamba.it

Planning Notes

  • Book Casa Perbellini and Famiglia Rana well in advance — weeks, not days
  • Three restaurants walkable in Verona centro: Perbellini, Iris, Il Desco
  • Oseleta and Amistà work best as part of a wine country or lake day
  • Damini and Casin del Gamba are destination drives — plan around them
  • All eight are doable in a long week if you're committed
